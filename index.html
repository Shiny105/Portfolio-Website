Introduction

A recent graduate with a Bachelor of Science in Computer Engineering and a minor in Mathematics from the University of Texas at Arlington, I bring a strong foundation in both hardaware and software aspects of computing. My academic projects, including a dual-channel waveform generator and a self-balancing robot, demonstrate my hands-on experience with embedded systems and FGPA design. Combined with my internship at Renesas Electronics, where I developed linear models for thermal resistance prediction, I offer a unique blend of theoretical knowledge and practical skills in areas such as RTL design, SoC development, and machine learning. I am excited to apply my diverse skill set, which spans languages like C++, Python, and System Verilog, to tackle challenging engineering problems and contribute to innovative solutions in the field of computer engineering.

Butterworth Low-Pass Filter

  • Applied Fast Fourier Transform (FFT) to convert time-domain audio signal to frequency domain. Identified noise components in 3500-6000 Hz range through normalized DFT magnitude plots.
  • Designed 17th order Butterworth lowpass filter with cutoff frequency ωc ≈ 0.60π. Calculated filter parameters using equations: 1 + (jωp / jΩc)^(2N) = 10^0.1 and 1 + (jωs / jΩc)^(2N) = 10^2.
  • Utilized MATLAB's butter() function to generate filter coefficients, then applied filter() function to process audio data. Implemented using difference equation: y[n] = b0x[n] + b1x[n-1] + ... - a1y[n-1] - a2y[n-2] - ...
  • Compared original and filtered DFT plots, demonstrating significant attenuation of frequencies above 3500 Hz. Resulting audio showed noticeable noise reduction while preserving speech intelligibility in lower frequency range.
